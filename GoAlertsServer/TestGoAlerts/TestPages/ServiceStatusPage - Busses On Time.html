General Train Information

 

 



The Quiet Zone

You asked, we listened! The Quiet Zone is now permanent on all rush hour trains Monday-Friday. To learn more about our Quiet Zone, click the following link: Welcome to the Quiet Zone.

The Canadian National Exhibition:
August 15th - September 1st

Come and GO to the CNE! Avoid the hassle of traffic and parking and take a relaxing train ride directly to the CNE. You can also purchase your tickets in advance at most GO Train stations, including Union, to avoid line ups at the CNE gates. Please visit our It's easy to GO to the CNE page for more information.

Schedule Changes
Effective August 30th

You asked, we listened. Starting August 30th, there will be more weekday bus trips between Niagara Falls and Burlington, a new Friday and Sunday bus route between University of Waterloo and York University and more weekend Hwy 407 East GO Bus service to serve you better. There will also be changes to some GO Bus services and minor time changes to many Lakeshore West and Kitchener train trips, including earlier departures. For details please see our Schedule Changes page.

Barrie

Newmarket GO Station boarding information:

Until winter 2014

Effective Wednesday, August 6th, phase 2 of construction begins on the platform. The work will extend and rehabilitate the platform, and provide a new ticket booth with a customer waiting area and improved accessibility features.

During this phase, the south half of the platform will be closed and there will be restricted train access – the doors will open on the five north coaches only. Construction will be completed in winter 2014.

Rutherford GO Station Shelter Rehabilitation:

Effective Monday July 28, the areas around the shelters along the bus platform will be hoarded off as we rehabilitate or replace them. Work will be done on one shelter at a time and is expected to be completed in approximately four weeks. 

Kitchener

Reduced Speeds – Expect Delays

GO Trains are travelling at reduced speeds between the Weston and Bloor GO Stations to allow for long-term service improvements as part of the ‘Big Move’. As a result, you may experience delays of up to 15 minutes to your commute. Please visit our Georgetown South Project page for details. We thank you for your patience.

 

Bloor GO Station - Train Boarding Information:

Effective until Early 2015

Due to construction, the east half of the north platform is closed and trains will stop further west of the platform. As a result, the five west end coaches will be off the west end of the north platform and passengers must use the five east end coaches only. See the Bloor GO Station page for more details.

 

Kitchener GO Station - Train Boarding Information:

Effective until Early 2015

Due to the Region of Waterloo road construction, the two west end coaches of the train will not open at this station. As a result, passengers must use the eight east end coaches located immediately behind the locomotive to board the train only. See the Kitchener GO Station page for more details.

 

Mount Pleasant GO Station - Infrastructure Improvements:

Effective until Fall 2014

As part of infrastructure improvements to your station, we are installing permanent water, storm water, sewer, and gas services. Customers will still be able to access Ashby Field Road from Bovaird by following the marked, temporary routes to reach the east and west parking lots, Kiss & Ride areas, and the station building. Access to the east parking lot from the eastern most access road has not been affected.

Please allow for extra time arriving at and departing from the station.

 

Weston GO Station - Train Boarding Information:

Effective until July 2014

On July 28th, 2014, we will begin boarding and exiting trains from the north side of the new centre platform. You will be able to use the existing ramp  from the parking lot to access the side platform and will then cross a bridge to the centre platform. Please exercise caution around the work site.

Lakeshore East

Pickering GO Station - Train Boarding Information:

Effective July 21, 2014 until Early 2015

Construction to install new elevators between the platform and the pedestrian bridge of platforms 1 & 2 at your station will begin. During this construction, the east-end of these platforms will be closed. Passengers will have to board and detrain from coaches on the west half of the train (coaches 5 and above) when using these platforms. 

 

Whitby GO Station - Construction on Henry St.

Effective August 11, 2014 until July 2015

Durham Region has started a long term construction project on Henry St. between Hwy 401 and Dundas St. (Hwy 2). The project is expected to finish in July of 2015. Due to the nature of the work, and the scheduled road closures, this may affect your normal route to the Whitby GO Station. We recommend that you give yourself extra travel time. For more information on this project please contact the Durham Works Department http://www.durham.ca/apps/works/cdeap/projectdetails.aspx?project=622.

Lakeshore West

Aldershot GO Station - Slow Traffic

The Ministry of Transportation has begun repairs on the Waterdown Road Bridge. While two – way traffic has been somewhat restricted, all ramps and exits remain open. Work is expected to be completed by the end of the summer. In the meantime we recommend that you allow for extra time entering and leaving the station.

Clarkson GO Station - Parking Lot Resurfacing 

Effective August 9th, 2014 until Fall 2014        

250 parking spots will be closed for resurfacing. The spots are near the new parking structure in the southeast lot.  Several carpool spaces will also be moved so we can work on the new access road which will improve traffic flow for the station. The Kiss & Ride and the accessible parking areas will remain open during the construction. Two electric vehicle charging stations are now available for use on the ground floor.

Milton

Lisgar GO Train Platform and Bus Loop Construction
July 2014 - Early 2015
We will be making several improvements to your train platform and bus loop,  including repairs, heated shelters and other upgrades. Most of this work will be completed during off-peak hours, however the accessible parking will be temporarily relocated to the north side of the station building. Construction is expected to be completed by early 2015. Please exercise caution around work areas.

Richmond Hill

GO Transit Continues to Work to Get You Home Safe and On-Time

With the winter weather behind us, we’d like to share some of the new things we’ve done to prepare for heavy rainfall so we can reduce the impact it has on GO Train service. After an official flood advisory is issued by the City of Toronto, or the Regional Conservation Authority, our staff and business partners are put on high-alert and all potential flood areas are thoroughly inspected. New storm-tracking software has also been installed in our Control Centre which monitors the weather and warns of forecasted storms. We’re testing flood detection sensors in some high risk areas and have installed three new culverts on the Richmond Hill line to help handle the run-off from city storm drains, which is a big help for rainy days we are sure to have.

Within the next two years, we’re looking to raise the tracks in the Don Valley section of our Richmond Hill line so they will be higher and have less chance of being flooded. We will keep you updated as we continue to look at other ways to limit the effects of heavy rainfall on our services.

GO Bus Delays on the Gardiner

The City of Toronto will be conducting major construction on the Gardiner Expressway, resulting in lane closures. This project will run until December 2016, with lanes re-opening temporarily in Summer 2015 for the Pan Am Games.  During this time, we are anticipating Bus delays of up to 20 minutes during peak hours on Routes 16, 21 and 31.  Buses should remain on-route, with slight detours as required. The City of Toronto's website provides valuable updates.

Lisgar GO Train Platform and Bus Loop Construction

Construction is expected to be complete by early 2015. Please exercise caution around work areas. 


Changes to Route 21 - Effective August 30th

GO Bus service between downtown Milton and Milton GO Station is being discontinued due to low ridership and there being Milton Transit options. Routes 21 & 27 will no longer service the stops between Milton GO and Main and Martin.

Reduced service on GO Bus Routes 21 and 27 requires the following stops to be removed: Martin Street @ Main Street, Millside Drive @ Martin Street and Main Street @ Court Street. In addition, the following stops will no longer be served: Main Street @ Wilson Drive and Main Street @ Ontario Street.

Milton Transit currently provides several options in this area on their routes 2, 5, 6, and 8 from Monday to Saturday. GO Bus passengers will be able to show their GO fare (tickets or PRESTO) and pay only $0.60 cents per ride on Milton Transit or a $24 monthly pass from Milton Transit is available. Customers can find out more about Milton Transit service by visiting www.miltontransit.ca or calling 905-864-4141.
