GO Trains are travelling at reduced speeds between the Weston and Bloor GO Stations to allow for long-term service improvements as part of the ‘Big Move’. Richmond Hill

GO Transit Continues to Work to Get You Home Safe and On-Time

With the winter weather behind us, we’d like to share some of the new things we’ve done to prepare for heavy rainfall so we can reduce the impact it has on GO Train service. After an official flood advisory is issued by the City of Toronto, or the Regional Conservation Authority, our staff and business partners are put on high-alert and all potential flood areas are thoroughly inspected. New storm-tracking software has also been installed in our Control Centre which monitors the weather and warns of forecasted storms. We’re testing flood detection sensors in some high risk areas and have installed three new culverts on the Richmond Hill line to help handle the run-off from city storm drains, which is a big help for rainy days we are sure to have.

Within the next two years, we’re looking to raise the tracks in the Don Valley section of our Richmond Hill line so they will be higher and have less chance of being flooded. We will keep you updated as we continue to look at other ways to limit the effects of heavy rainfall on our services.

The Route 92 eastbound stop on Kingston Road at Port Union has been moved back to its original location. Passenger may once again board using the southeast side at the newly designed bus bay and waiting area.

98 - Pickering / Finch

Changes to Route 98 - Effective September 2nd

GO Bus Route 92A will replace GO Bus Route 98 and provide more service to Ajax and West Pickering, but will not operate between Pickering GO Station and Liverpool Road/Pickering Parkway. These changes will begin Tuesday, September 2nd.

GO Bus 92A will not operate from Pickering GO, but will stay on Highway 2 providing express versions of these trips with 9 westbound morning rush hour trips and 10 eastbound afternoon rush hour trips. Durham Region Transit provides several bus routes to/from Pickering GO to Highway 2 where passengers will be able to connect to Bus 92A. PRESTO card holders will pay only $0.70 when transferring between GO and DRT. Customers can find out more about DRT service by visiting http://durhamregiontransit.com or calling 1-866-247-0055.

General Elevator Information

UNION STATION:

  • The bridge connecting Union Station and the Union Station Bus Terminal is out of service for approximately one year. Passengers with accessible needs are asked to see GO staff, who will escort you safely to and from the Union Station GO Concourse.

Lakeshore East

AJAX GO:

  • The east tunnel has been reopened; however, the elevator remains out of service. Customers requiring an elevator are advised to continue using the west tunnel.    

PICKERING GO:

  • The elevator connecting the parking structure to the pedestrian bridge is out of service. The station remains accessible.

Lakeshore West

BURLINGTON GO:

  • The elevator connecting the tunnel to track #1 is out of service. Passengers requiring the use of this elevator to access the platform may use the walkway at the west end of the platform that connects to the north bus loop and parking structure.
  • There will be no elevator access from the south side of the station until early 2015. Construction at Burlington will limit elevator access to the platforms until 2015.
